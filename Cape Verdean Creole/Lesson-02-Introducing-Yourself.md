# Useful Sentences

## 1. My name is Maria.

**Creole:** N ta txoma Maria.

**Pronunciation:** n tah CHOH-mah mah-REE-ah

**Meaning:** My name is Maria.

### Word Breakdown

- **N** = I
- **ta** = (present tense marker)
- **txoma** = am called
- **Maria** = Maria

---

## 2. What is your name?

**Creole:** Modi bu ta txoma?

**Pronunciation:** MOH-dee boo tah CHOH-mah

**Meaning:** What is your name?

### Word Breakdown

- **Modi** = How / What
- **bu** = you
- **ta** = (present tense marker)
- **txoma** = are called

---

## 3. I am João.

**Creole:** N sta João.

**Pronunciation:** n stah zhoo-OW

**Meaning:** I am João.

### Word Breakdown

- **N** = I
- **sta** = am
- **João** = João

---

## 4. Yes.

## 5. No.

# Grammar Tip

In Cape Verdean Creole, people often say:

**N ta txoma...**

This literally means **"I am called..."**, but it is used just like **"My name is..."** in English.

---

# Culture Tip

When meeting someone in Cape Verde, it's common to greet them first before asking their name. A friendly **"Oi!"** or **"Bon dia!"** followed by **"Modi bu ta txoma?"** is considered polite.
